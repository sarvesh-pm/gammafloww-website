Choosing a white-label provider is a multi-year commitment that's expensive to reverse. A demo looks great; the risks live in what the demo doesn't show. Use this checklist to pressure-test every vendor before you sign.

## 1. Technology

- **Matching engine performance** — throughput and latency under load, not just a quiet demo. Ask for benchmarks. See [inside a matching engine](/blog/inside-a-crypto-matching-engine).
- **Risk & liquidation** — how the [liquidation engine and insurance fund](/blog/risk-and-liquidation-engines) behave in a fast market.
- **Products & roadmap** — does it support what you'll grow into ([spot, perps, options](/blog/perpetual-futures-vs-options-vs-dated-futures)), or will you re-platform?
- **Scalability** — proven volume ceilings, and what happens at 10× your launch load.

## 2. Security

- Independent audits and penetration tests (recent, with reports you can read).
- Custody model and key management — see [custody models](/blog/crypto-exchange-custody-models).
- Incident history and disclosure practices. Ask directly: "What's the worst outage you've had, and what changed after?"

## 3. Liquidity

- Bundled liquidity vs. bring-your-own, and the cost of each.
- Market-maker relationships and how the [cold-start](/blog/crypto-exchange-liquidity-cold-start) problem is handled.

## 4. Compliance

- KYC/AML tooling and vendors ([KYC/AML for exchanges](/blog/kyc-aml-for-crypto-exchanges)).
- Jurisdictions supported, and whether the platform fits your target [licensing regime](/blog/crypto-derivatives-licensing-global-guide).
- Reporting, surveillance, and audit-trail capabilities.

## 5. Commercials

- Pricing model and true cost of ownership — decode it with our [pricing-models guide](/blog/white-label-exchange-pricing-models).
- What's included vs. billed separately, and any volume caps or minimums.

<Callout title="The clause everyone forgets: exit">
Ask what happens when you *leave*. Who owns the data? Can you export users, balances, and history? What's the notice period, and is there technical lock-in? A provider confident in their product will have clean answers.
</Callout>

## 6. Support & references

- SLA specifics: uptime guarantee, response times, escalation paths, and penalties for breach.
- Talk to **current** clients — ideally ones at your scale — and ask what they'd change.

## Score every vendor the same way

The value of a checklist is consistency. Run all six sections on every shortlisted provider and score them side by side; the real differences surface fast — and they're rarely the headline price. This pairs with [how to choose a white-label provider](/blog/how-to-choose-white-label-exchange-provider) and the full [launch cost breakdown](/blog/cost-to-launch-crypto-derivatives-exchange).

## The takeaway

Diligence is cheap; switching providers isn't. Interrogate technology, security, liquidity, compliance, commercials, and exit terms with the same rubric for every vendor — and weight the answers you have to dig for most heavily.
