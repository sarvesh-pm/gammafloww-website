One of the first strategic decisions a new operator faces is deceptively simple: spot, derivatives, or both? They share infrastructure but differ sharply in cost, complexity, revenue, and regulation. Here's how to choose.

## Where the volume — and the money — is

On centralized venues, derivatives dominate: they account for roughly **76.5% of trading volume** versus spot's 23.5% ([CCData](https://data.coindesk.com/reports/exchange-review-march-2026)).

<BarChart id="derivatives-vs-spot" />

Yet among *white-label* buyers, **spot still holds about 62.6% of demand** ([Blockonomi](https://blockonomi.com/the-review-of-white-label-crypto-exchanges-market-data-and-trends-2026/)) — because it's the faster, cheaper, lower-risk way to get to market. The gap between where volume is and what new operators launch is the whole tension.

## Spot vs. derivatives at a glance

| | Spot | Derivatives |
|---|---|---|
| **Complexity** | Lower | High — [matching](/blog/inside-a-crypto-matching-engine), [risk & liquidation](/blog/risk-and-liquidation-engines), funding |
| **Revenue per trade** | Lower fees | Higher — leverage drives volume & [fees](/blog/how-crypto-exchanges-make-money) |
| **Capital & licensing** | Lighter | Heavier (derivatives regimes) |
| **Liquidity need** | Order-book depth | Depth + a liquidation/insurance backstop |
| **Time to launch** | Fastest | Longer |

## How to decide

- **Launch spot first if** you want the fastest, lowest-risk path to a live product, a simpler compliance footprint, and time to build an audience before taking on leverage mechanics.
- **Launch derivatives first if** your edge, audience, or business model is built around leverage — that's where the [volume and margins concentrate](/blog/how-crypto-exchanges-make-money), and it's what most traders actually use.
- **Do both** only if you have the team and capital to run two risk profiles at once. Most don't on day one.

<Callout title="Sequencing beats simultaneity">
A common, low-risk path: launch spot to establish brand and liquidity, then add [perpetuals](/blog/perpetual-futures-vs-options-vs-dated-futures) once you have traders and operational maturity. The catch — make sure your platform can grow into derivatives without a re-platform. Ask a [provider](/blog/how-to-choose-white-label-exchange-provider) whether the same engine supports both.
</Callout>

## The infrastructure angle

Spot and derivatives share a matching engine, but derivatives layer on margining, funding, a [liquidation engine, and an insurance fund](/blog/risk-and-liquidation-engines) — materially more to build and run. If you plan to reach derivatives eventually, choosing a stack that already supports it avoids an expensive migration later. That's a core part of the [build-vs-buy](/blog/white-label-vs-building-crypto-exchange) calculus.

## The takeaway

Derivatives own the volume; spot owns the easy on-ramp. Pick spot first for speed and simplicity, derivatives first if leverage is your business — but either way, launch on infrastructure that can carry you into the other without starting over.
