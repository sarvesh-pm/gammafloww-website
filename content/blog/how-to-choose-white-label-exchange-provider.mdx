Once you've decided to go white-label, the next decision is which provider — and it matters enormously. The engine you pick determines your traders' experience, your uptime, and how fast you can grow. There's a crowded field of providers ([Finance Magnates](https://www.financemagnates.com/cryptocurrency/top-white-label-crypto-exchange-providers-of-2026/)), so here's a buyer's checklist to cut through it.

## 1. Liquidity — the make-or-break factor

Ask exactly *how* liquidity is provided and how deep the books are on the pairs you'll run. Thin books lose users in seconds (see [the cold-start problem](/blog/crypto-exchange-liquidity-cold-start)). A strong provider aggregates real depth so you launch deep, not empty.

## 2. Engine performance

Your [matching engine](/blog/inside-a-crypto-matching-engine) must be fast, deterministic, and reliable under load. Ask for:

- **Latency** figures and **throughput** (orders/sec) under stress.
- Behavior during volatility spikes and historical uptime.
- Supported **order types** (stop, post-only, reduce-only) — pros need them.

## 3. Risk & liquidation systems

For a leveraged venue, the [risk engine](/blog/risk-and-liquidation-engines) is safety-critical. Confirm how liquidation, the insurance fund, and auto-deleveraging work, and what margin modes ([isolated and cross](/blog/isolated-vs-cross-margin)) and leverage tiers are supported.

## 4. Compliance & jurisdiction support

The provider won't hold your licence, but it should support your obligations: KYC/AML integrations, Travel Rule tooling, and configurability for your [jurisdiction's rules](/blog/crypto-derivatives-licensing-global-guide). Ask which regulatory regimes their partners already operate under.

## 5. Customization & ownership

How much is truly yours? Branding and UI should be fully white-labeled. Check how much you can configure ([fees](/blog/maker-taker-fees-explained), pairs, leverage, risk limits) versus what's locked, and clarify data ownership and exit terms.

## 6. The real cost model

Get the **total** cost, not the headline: setup fee, monthly/revenue share, and what's excluded (liquidity, custom features, support tiers). Providers often charge extra for anything outside the template ([B2Broker](https://b2broker.com/news/white-label-crypto-exchange-cost/)). See [cost breakdown](/blog/cost-to-launch-crypto-derivatives-exchange).

## 7. Support & SLAs

You're trusting this partner with live markets. Confirm 24/7 support, SLA-backed uptime, escalation paths, and how quickly they ship fixes and features.

<Callout title="Quick scorecard">
Rate each provider 1–5 on: **liquidity depth**, **engine performance**, **risk systems**, **compliance support**, **customization**, **total cost**, and **support/SLAs**. A cheap provider that fails on liquidity or reliability is the most expensive choice you can make.
</Callout>

## The takeaway

Choose on liquidity, engine reliability, and risk systems first — those are what your traders feel and what you can't easily fix later. Price matters, but it's the last filter, not the first. The right provider lets you compete on brand and growth while the engine just works.
