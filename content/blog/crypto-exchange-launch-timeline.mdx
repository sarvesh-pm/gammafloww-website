"How long until we're live?" On white-label infrastructure, the answer is weeks, not the 12–18 months a from-scratch build takes. But that headline hides an important nuance: the *technical* launch is fast, while *licensing* runs on its own, longer clock. Here's a realistic timeline.

## The big picture

<BarChart id="time-to-launch" />

The technical build compresses to **4–8 weeks** on a white-label engine ([Codono](https://codono.com/blog/white-label-crypto-exchange-vs-custom-build), [TinyTrader](https://tinytrader.com/how-much-does-a-white-label-crypto-exchange-cost-in-2026/)). The catch: licensing usually takes longer and should start first — see below.

## A realistic week-by-week

This assumes your licensing track is already underway (more on that next):

- **Weeks 1–2 — Setup & integration.** Provision the platform, connect front end to APIs, stand up the backend and market data.
- **Weeks 2–3 — Branding & customization.** Apply your brand and UI, configure trading pairs, leverage tiers, and your [fee schedule](/blog/maker-taker-fees-explained).
- **Weeks 3–4 — Compliance wiring.** Integrate [KYC/AML](/blog/kyc-aml-for-crypto-exchanges), deposits/withdrawals, and Travel Rule tooling.
- **Weeks 4–6 — Testing.** Validate edge cases end-to-end — order types, [liquidations](/blog/how-liquidation-price-is-calculated), funding — and confirm [liquidity](/blog/crypto-exchange-liquidity-cold-start) depth.
- **Weeks 6–8 — Soft launch → go-live.** Limited users first, then public launch with SLA-backed support and monitoring.

<Callout title="The real gating item: licensing">
The 4–8 week clock is the *technical* timeline. [Licensing](/blog/crypto-derivatives-licensing-global-guide) typically takes months and varies by jurisdiction — so start it first and run it in parallel. Teams that treat licensing as a final step, not a first one, are the teams that slip.
</Callout>

## What determines where you land in the range

- **Customization depth** — a lightly-branded deploy is faster than a heavily-customized one.
- **Product scope** — perps-first is quicker than launching perps + options at once (see [what to launch first](/blog/perpetual-futures-vs-options-vs-dated-futures)).
- **Provider & readiness** — how much your [chosen provider](/blog/how-to-choose-white-label-exchange-provider) pre-integrates, and how prepared your compliance materials are.

## The takeaway

Technically, a white-label derivatives venue goes from concept to live in 4–8 weeks. Realistically, licensing is the long pole — start it first, run the build in parallel, and you can be trading in a couple of months instead of a couple of years.
