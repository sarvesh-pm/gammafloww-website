Hong Kong has positioned itself as a regulated gateway for virtual assets, and its VATP (Virtual Asset Trading Platform) regime is now maturing. If you're weighing Hong Kong as a base, the key thing to understand is that its framework is built on *securities* law — which changes everything for a derivatives venue.

## The dual framework

Hong Kong's regime rests on two ordinances supervised by the **Securities and Futures Commission (SFC)**:

| Ordinance | Governs |
|---|---|
| **SFO** (Securities and Futures Ordinance) | Financial instruments, securities, and derivatives |
| **AMLO** (Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance) | AML/CFT obligations for VASPs |

The VASP/VATP provisions took effect on **1 June 2023**, and as of early 2026 the SFC had licensed **13 VATPs** ([Fintech Hong Kong](https://fintechnews.hk/licensed-crypto-exchanges-hong-kong/), [CryptoSlate](https://cryptoslate.com/crypto-laws/hong-kong-vasp-licensing-regime/)).

## Why derivatives are different here

Under the SFO, derivatives and security tokens are regulated as **securities** — so a platform touching even a single security token needs **both a Type 1 (dealing in securities) and a Type 7 (providing automated trading services) licence** ([CryptoSlate](https://cryptoslate.com/crypto-laws/hong-kong-vasp-licensing-regime/)). This is a different, heavier permission set than a spot-only VA platform.

<Callout title="Derivatives & margin: under active review">
Retail access to virtual-asset derivatives has been tightly restricted in Hong Kong. In early 2026 the SFC announced new regimes for OTC trading alongside a **review of derivatives and margin trading** for virtual assets, and Hong Kong regulators are targeting 2026 legislation for VA dealer and custodian rules ([CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/policy/2025/12/25/hong-kong-regulators-target-2026-legislation-for-virtual-asset-dealer-and-custodian-rules)). If derivatives are your product, this is the space to watch — the rules are still forming.
</Callout>

## Client-asset and conduct standards

The SFC's VATP framework is prescriptive on operations: token due diligence, custody standards, cybersecurity, and **client-asset segregation**. Notably, SFC-regulated VA dealers may only safekeep client virtual assets with **SFC-regulated VA custodian service providers** — custody isn't something you can improvise (see [custody models](/blog/crypto-exchange-custody-models)).

## What it means for operators

- **Expect securities-grade licensing.** A derivatives or security-token venue needs Type 1 + Type 7, not a light-touch VA registration.
- **Plan around an evolving derivatives regime.** Build on infrastructure flexible enough to adapt as the SFC finalises derivatives/margin rules.
- **Custody is regulated separately.** Line up an SFC-regulated custodian early.

Compare Hong Kong with the [EU (MiCA)](/blog/mica-crypto-derivatives-casp-license), [UAE (VARA)](/blog/uae-vara-crypto-derivatives-license), and [Singapore (MAS)](/blog/singapore-mas-crypto-derivatives-license) routes in our [global licensing guide](/blog/crypto-derivatives-licensing-global-guide).

## The takeaway

Hong Kong regulates virtual-asset derivatives as securities under the SFO, requiring Type 1 + Type 7 licences and SFC-regulated custody — and the specific rules for derivatives and margin are still being written in 2026. Promising, but plan for a securities-grade bar and a moving target.
