If you want to launch a crypto futures or options venue, you have two paths: build the entire exchange yourself, or license a **white-label** platform and put your brand on proven infrastructure. This guide covers what white-label really means, how the split of responsibilities works, what it costs, and when it's the right call.

## What "white-label" actually means

A white-label crypto derivatives exchange is a ready-built trading platform you operate under your own brand. The provider runs the hard engineering underneath — the matching engine, liquidity, and risk systems — while you own the customer-facing business.

The cleanest way to understand it is the division of labor:

- **You own:** brand and UI, deposits/withdrawals, KYC/AML and compliance, fee schedules, growth and support.
- **The provider runs:** the [matching engine](/blog/inside-a-crypto-matching-engine), [liquidity](/blog/crypto-exchange-liquidity-cold-start), the [risk and liquidation engine](/blog/risk-and-liquidation-engines), and 24/7 infrastructure.

## Why derivatives, specifically

Because that's where the market is. As of March 2026, derivatives made up **76.5%** of centralized-exchange trading volume ([CCData](https://data.coindesk.com/reports/exchange-review-march-2026)). A spot-only venue leaves the majority of activity on the table. (See [How Crypto Exchanges Make Money](/blog/how-crypto-exchanges-make-money).)

## Is white-label right for you?

<Callout title="A simple rule">
Build only if your differentiator *is* the engine — you're doing something genuinely novel at the matching/risk layer and have the capital and 12–18 months to do it. For nearly every other case — a regional brokerage, a fintech adding crypto, a brand monetizing an audience — white-label is faster, cheaper, and safer.
</Callout>

White-label fits when:

- Your edge is **brand, audience, or region**, not core exchange tech.
- You want to **test a market** without a multi-million, multi-year commitment.
- You need **institutional-grade reliability** from day one without building it.

## How the launch works

The typical path runs consultation → requirements → integration → customization → testing → go-live, in roughly 4–8 weeks. Full walkthrough: [How to Launch a Crypto Derivatives Exchange](/blog/how-to-launch-a-crypto-derivatives-exchange). Remember you still own [licensing](/blog/crypto-derivatives-licensing-global-guide) and [KYC/AML](/blog/kyc-aml-for-crypto-exchanges) — white-label handles the engine, not your regulatory obligations.

## The takeaway

A white-label derivatives exchange lets you own the brand and the revenue while a provider runs the engine — launching in weeks instead of years. It's the default choice unless your competitive edge lives in the infrastructure itself.
