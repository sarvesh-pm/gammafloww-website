Every white-label provider quotes differently, which makes apples-to-apples comparison hard. Underneath the sales decks, though, there are only a handful of pricing structures. Knowing them lets you see past the headline number to the true cost of ownership.

## The four models

| Model | How you pay | Best when |
|---|---|---|
| **Setup + monthly licence** | One-time build fee, then a fixed monthly/annual fee | You want predictable costs and expect high volume |
| **Revenue share** | Provider takes a % of trading fees / revenue | You want low upfront cost and to share risk |
| **SaaS subscription** | Tiered recurring fee by usage/features | You want to start small and scale tiers |
| **Hybrid** | Lower setup + smaller rev-share, or licence + overage | Most real deals land here |

Most providers blend these — a modest setup fee plus either a licence or a revenue share. The market is large and competitive (the crypto exchange market is projected around **$85.75B in 2026** — [Blockonomi](https://blockonomi.com/the-review-of-white-label-crypto-exchanges-market-data-and-trends-2026/)), so terms are negotiable.

## The trade-off that actually matters

The core tension is **upfront cost vs. long-term margin**:

- **Revenue share** minimises what you pay on day one but scales with your success — at high volume, you may pay far more over time than a flat licence would have cost.
- **Fixed licence** costs more early but caps your platform expense, so more of each incremental trading fee is yours.

Where the crossover sits depends on your projected volume and fee schedule. Because a big share of exchange revenue comes from [derivatives trading fees](/blog/how-crypto-exchanges-make-money) and [maker-taker spreads](/blog/maker-taker-fees-explained), model your expected volume before choosing — a revenue-share deal that looks cheap can quietly become your largest cost line.

## What drives the number

- **Products** — spot is cheaper; [derivatives](/blog/perpetual-futures-vs-options-vs-dated-futures) (perps, options) cost more to license and run.
- **Liquidity** — bundled liquidity vs. bring-your-own materially changes both price and your [cold-start](/blog/crypto-exchange-liquidity-cold-start) risk.
- **Compliance scope** — KYC/AML, jurisdictions, and reporting add cost.
- **Customisation** — off-the-shelf skin vs. deep brand/UX and feature work.
- **Support & SLAs** — uptime guarantees and response times are priced in.

For the full build-out budget beyond the platform fee, see [what it costs to launch a crypto derivatives exchange](/blog/cost-to-launch-crypto-derivatives-exchange).

## Questions to ask every provider

1. Is this setup + licence, revenue share, or hybrid — and at what volume does one beat the other?
2. What exactly is included vs. billed separately (liquidity, data, custody, compliance vendors)?
3. Are there volume caps, overage fees, or minimums?
4. What's the SLA, and what does exceeding support/change limits cost?
5. What are the exit terms — data portability, notice period, lock-in?

Use the same list on every vendor and the real cost differences surface fast. This pairs with our guide on [how to choose a white-label provider](/blog/how-to-choose-white-label-exchange-provider).

## The takeaway

Pricing comes down to setup-plus-licence, revenue share, SaaS, or a hybrid — and the right choice hinges on your projected volume and what's bundled. Model the volume, itemise the add-ons, and compare true cost of ownership, not the headline quote.
